01

Meet Safely

The first meeting matters most. A few habits make it safer, every time.

Meet in public places.Choose a busy café, restaurant, or public space, at least for your first few dates.
Tell a friend where you're going.Share who you're meeting, and when and where, before you go.
Arrange your own transportation.Get yourself to and from the date. Don't rely on a stranger for a ride.
Keep your phone charged.A dead battery is the one thing you can't afford to plan around.
Trust your instincts.If something feels wrong, that feeling is reason enough to leave.
02

Online Safety

Everything that happens after "hello" should happen on your terms.

Never send money.Not to someone you haven't met in person, no matter the story.
Never share passwords.Not for SnapMate, your email, your bank, or anything else.
Avoid suspicious links.Don't click links sent by people you don't know well yet.
Verify identities before meeting.A quick video call confirms the person matches their profile.
Don't rush off SnapMate.Pressure to move to WhatsApp or texting right away is a common scam tactic. Take your time.
03

Common Scams

Know the pattern and the story loses its power. Tap each one to read how it works.

Romance Scam＋
A stranger builds a fast, intense emotional bond, then invents a crisis, medical, legal, or travel, that only money can fix.
Crypto Scam＋
Someone convinces you to "invest" through a crypto platform or wallet they control, then disappears with your funds.
Investment Scam＋
A promise of guaranteed, unusually high returns on an opportunity that doesn't actually exist.
Gift Card Scam＋
A request to pay a debt, fee, or "verification" cost using gift cards instead of a traceable payment method. Real institutions never ask for this.
Fake Emergency Scam＋
A sudden, urgent story, a stranded relative, an unpaid hospital bill, designed to make you act before you think.
Blackmail & Sextortion＋
Someone threatens to share private photos or messages unless you pay or comply. Stop responding and report it, don't engage or pay.
04

Privacy

What you share is yours to control.

Protect your personal information.Keep your last name, address, and workplace off your profile.
Hide sensitive details.Location, employer, and daily routine are easy things to leave out.
Review your profile before publishing.Check every photo and every line of your bio before it goes live.
Use strong, unique passwords.A password manager makes this effortless, and worth setting up once.
05

Report & Block

Two different tools. Here's when to reach for each.

Report

Flags a member for our Safety Team to review. We look into every report and take action, from a warning to a permanent ban, based on what we find.

USE WHEN: behavior breaks our guidelines, even if it doesn't affect you directly.

Block

Immediately prevents another member from contacting you or seeing your profile. It takes effect the moment you tap it, no review needed.

USE WHEN: you want someone gone from your experience, right now.
You can do both at once. Blocking protects you instantly; reporting helps us protect everyone else.

Healthy Relationships

Good connections are built on these, from the first message onward.

Respect boundaries."No" is a complete sentence, and deserves to be treated like one.
Communicate honestly.Say what you mean, and ask about what you don't understand.
Consent matters.At every step, every time, with no exceptions.
Recognize red flags.Love-bombing, jealousy, and control are warning signs, not compliments.
Avoid manipulation.Guilt, pressure, and ultimatums have no place in a healthy relationship.
07

Emergency Help

If you ever feel unsafe, act first. Figure out the rest later.

Leave immediately.Trust the feeling over the awkwardness of leaving.
Contact local emergency services.911 in the US, 112 across the EU and UK, or your local equivalent.
Tell a trusted friend.Call or message someone right away, even a short update helps.
Report the account to SnapMate.Our Safety Team reviews every report and can act quickly.
EMERGENCY NUMBERS VARY BY COUNTRY — WHEN IN DOUBT, CALL LOCAL POLICE.
